Uniform Circular Motion

  • Uniform circular motion (UCM) is a type of motion where an object travels in a circle with constant speed
  • There is an acceleration since the direction of the velocity is changing
  • This acceleration is called centripetal acceleration
  • For uniform circular motion, the position can be described using sine and cosine
    • AAA﻿ is the magnitude of the position vector; radius of rotation
    • A=∣r(t)∣A = |r(t)|A=∣r(t)∣﻿
    • ω\omegaω﻿ is the angular frequency of the object; amount of radians that the object travels in 1 second
    • TTT﻿ is the period; time for 1 full revolution
  • The velocity and acceleration function can be found by differentiation
  • Circular motion can be nonuniform
  • In nonuniform circular motion, there is an additional acceleration tangent to the circle; called tangential acceleration (aTa_TaT​﻿)
  • Tangential acceleration is the rate of change of the magnitude of the velocity with respect to time
  • Thus, a particle in circular motion has a total acceleration that is the sum of the centripetal and tangential accelerations
  • To find the sum, you can treat each vector as a magnitude and find the magnitude of the resultant vector and the angle
  • The angle from tangent to the circle is the arctan of the centripetal acceleration to the tangential acceleration
