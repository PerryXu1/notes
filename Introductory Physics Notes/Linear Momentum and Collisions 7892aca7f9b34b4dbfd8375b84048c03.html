Linear Momentum and Collisions

Linear Momentum

  • Momentum (p⃗\vec{p}p​﻿) is a measure of mass in motion
    • Characterizes “quantity of motion”
    • Momentum is a vector quantity
    • Often used to determine how easy/hard it is to change an object’s motion
  • As velocity changes, so does momentum
  • Change in the momentum of an object is proportional to the force applied as well as the time applied for

  • The impulse-momentum theorem states that impulse is equal to change in momentum
  • Problem solving strategy for impulse-momentum theorem
    • Express the impulse in terms of force and time

    • Only applies when mass is constant

Conservation of Linear Momentum

  • The Law of Conservation of Momentum states that the total momentum of a closed system is conserved
  • Conditions for conservation of momentum
    • Mass remains constant
    • There is no net external force
  • A system is a collection of relevant objects
  • A system where mass is constant and there is no net external force is called a closed system (AKA isolated system)
  • Problem solving strategy for conservation of momentum
    • Identify a closed system (constant mass, zero net external force)
    • Write down expressions representing the total momentum before and after the event
    • Equate them and solve

Types of Collisions

  • Types of collisions
    • Explosion
      • One object becomes many
      • The kinetic energy of the system is increased
    • Inelastic collision
      • Two or more objects collide with each other and kinetic energy decreases
      • A totally inelastic collision is one where the objects stick together and the loss of kinetic energy is a maximum
    • Elastic collision
      • Kinetic energy is conserved
  • Problem solving strategy for collisions
    • Define a closed system
    • Write down an expression for conservation of momentum
    • If kinetic energy is conserved, write down an expression for conservation of kinetic energy
      • Else, write down the expression for the change in kinetic energy
    • Solve the system of two equations
      • One solution shows the velocities after the collision (correct one) and one shows the velocities if the objects miss (velocities are the same)

Collisions in Multiple Dimensions

  • In two or three dimensions, split the momentum into x, y, and z components and solve independently
  • Problem solving strategy for conservation of momentum in multiple dimensions
    • Identify a closed system
    • Write down an equation that represents conservation of momentum in the x direction and solve for the desired quantity
    • Write down an equation that represents conservation of momentum in the y direction and solve for the desired quantity
    • Write down an equation that represents conservation of momentum in the z direction and solve for the desired quantity
