Calculating Moments of Inertia

  • For continuous objects, an integral is needed to calculate the moment of inertia
    • dmdmdm﻿ must be stated in terms of a mass density and a characteristic dimension
    • If the density can be expressed as kilograms per meter, it has a linear mass density and is given the symbol λ\lambdaλ﻿
      • λ\lambdaλ﻿ can be computed as mass/length
    • If the density can be expressed as kilograms per square metre, it has a surface mass density and is given the symbol σ\sigmaσ﻿
      • σ\sigmaσ﻿ can be computed as mass/area
  • A parallel axis is an axis parallel to the axis through the center of mass
    • For example, for a 1 dimensional rod, the axis of rotation through the center is a parallel axis to an axis of rotation at 1 end of the rod
  • The parallel axis theorem is a theorem that simplifies determining the moment of inertia for a rod about any parallel axis
    • The moment of inertia of an object rotating about any parallel axis is equal to the moment of inertia of the object rotating about the center of mass plus the mass times the distance between the axes squared
  • The total moment of inertia is the sum of all individual moments of inertia about a common axis

  • The unit of torque is newton metres (N⋅m\text{N} \cdot \text{m}N⋅m﻿)
  • Problem solving strategy for finding net torque
    • Choose a coordinate system with the axis of rotation as the origin
    • Determine the angle between the lever arm vector r and the force vector F
    • Take the cross product of r and F to find the torque
    • Sum the torques to find the net torque
