Alternating-Current Circuits

AC Sources

  • AC (alternating current) is the type of current used to deliver electricity to our homes though transmission lines
    • Minimizes heating losses in transmission lines
  • Most well-known applications use AC

Resonance in an AC Circuit

  • We can vary the frequency of the AC generator while keeping the peak voltage constant to see how the peak current changes
  • The peak current reaches its maximum when the AC generator reaches its resonant frequency f0f_0f0​﻿
    • This corresponds to the resonant angular frequency ω0=2πf0\omega_0 = 2\pi f_0ω0​=2πf0​﻿
    • The resonant angular frequency is the angular frequency that minimizes the impedance ZZZ﻿
  • At the resonant angular frequency, the impedance is entirely resistive, and the emf and current are in phase
  • At resonance, the impedance is minimized to just the resistance, so the average power is maximized
  • In a graph of PaveP_{ave}Pave​﻿ vs ω\omegaω﻿ around the maximum power output region, the bandwidth Δω\Delta \omegaΔω﻿ of the resonance peak is the range of angular frequencies ω\omegaω﻿ such that the power is greater than one-half of the maximum value of PaveP_{ave}Pave​﻿
  • The sharpness of the resonance peak is described by the dimensionless quality factor QQQ﻿
  • The higher the value of QQQ﻿, the sharper the peak
  • QQQ﻿ can be given in terms of the circuit parameters
  • Resonant circuits are commonly used to pass or reject certain frequency ranges
    • The circuit can be “tuned to a particular resonant frequency by adjusting the circuit parameters
    • If a circuit has a high quality factor, it will have a small bandwidth, so signals at even slightly different frequencies will not be passed by the circuit due to high impedance

Transformers

  • AC power is produced at relatively low voltages, but it’s sent through transmission lines at very high voltages
    • Can be as high as 500 kV
  • There are two ways to meet a power transmission requirement: high voltage and low current, or low voltage and high current
  • Using high voltage and low current results is lower ohmic losses in the transmission lines, sincePave=Irms2RP_{ave} = I_{rms}^2 RPave​=Irms2​R﻿
    • Resistance can be great due to very long transmission lines
  • Therefore, emfs must be stepped up to high emfs for transmission and stepped down to low emfs for use in households
  • The device used for transforming voltages from one value to another is the transformer
    • Uses induction
  • In basis, a transformer consists of two separate coils (AKA windings) that are wrapped around a soft iron core
    • The windings are labelled as primary PPP﻿ and secondary SSS﻿
    • The primary winding has NPN_PNP​﻿ loops and is connected to an alternating voltage vp(t)v_p(t)vp​(t)﻿
    • The secondary windings has NSN_SNS​﻿ turns and is connected to a load resistor RSR_SRS​﻿
    • In the ideal case, all magnetic field lines are within the iron core so each turn in each winding gets the same magnetic flux
    • Energy loss due to hysteresis, ohmic heating in the windings and ohmic heating in the core due to eddy currents are also all neglected
      • Not a terrible assumption as transformers are pretty efficient
  • In a transformer, the ratio of voltages between the windings is equal to the ratio of the turns in each winding, as described by the transformer equation
    • Voltage is “stepped up” when NS>NPN_S > N_PNS​>NP​﻿
    • Voltage is “stepped down” when NS<NPN_S < N_PNS​<NP​﻿
  • Current is also modified by the ratio of the primary turns to secondary turns
  • Resistance between the two windings is modified by the ratio of the turns squared
