Rolling Motion

  • When an object rolls without slipping, the bottom of the object is stationary with respect to the ground
  • Static friction is needed to keep an object rolling without slipping
    • The force of static friction must be less than or equal to the coefficient of static friction times the normal force
    • The force of static friction should be written in terms of the moment of inertia and independent of the coefficient of friction
  • When an object rolls without slipping, the motion of the center of mass is equal to the tangential motion
  • The acceleration of an object rolling without slipping on a slope
  • When an object rolls with slipping, the bottom is not stationary relative to the ground
  • Kinetic friction is needed to make an object roll when its slipping
    • Kinetic friction is written in terms of the coefficient of kinetic friction
  • When an object rolls with slipping down an inclined plane, the linear acceleration is the same for a sliding object
  • The angular acceleration of an object rolling with slipping can be found using Newton’s second law for rotation
  • A rolling object has rotational kinetic energy in addition to translational kinetic energy and potential energy
  • This mechanical energy is conserved
  • In order to solve for some quantities, the angular velocity should be rewritten in terms of the linear velocity
