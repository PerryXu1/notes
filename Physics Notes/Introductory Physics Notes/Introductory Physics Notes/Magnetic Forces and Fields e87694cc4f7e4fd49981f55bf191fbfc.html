  • The magnetic field can be represented by magnetic field lines
  • Each magnetic field line must form a closed loop
    • Loop could be completed out of the drawing
  • Rules for drawing magnetic field lines:
    1. Magnetic field lines must emerge from the north pole, loop around to the south pole and continue back to the north pole
      • No magnetic monopoles can exist
    1. The direction of the magnetic field is tangent to the field line at any point in space
      • A compass will point tangent to the field line
    1. The strength of the magnetic field is proportional to the closeness of the lines
      • Exactly proportional to lines per unit area perpendicular to the lines
    1. Magnetic field lines never cross
    1. Magnetic field lines are continuous

Force and Torque on a Current Loop

  • The magnetic force on any closed current loop in a uniform magnetic field is always zero
    • There may be torque though

    • When a current is run through the loops, the magnetic field exerts a torque on the loops, which rotates a shaft
    • Electrical energy converted to mechanical work

The Hall Effect

  • In a metallic strip of width lll﻿ in a constant magnetic field, when elections are moving from left to right, the magnetic force pushes them to the bottom edge
    • This makes the top positive and the bottom negative, resulting in an electric field from top to bottom
    • This builds up until the magnetic force on the electrons balance the electric field on the electrons
    • This effect can be used to determine the drift speed of electrons in the metal
  • Can be used to make a velocity selector
